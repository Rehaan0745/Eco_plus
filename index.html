The Closed-Loop Bio-Ecosystem

How corn-derived compounds transition seamlessly back to nature.

Phase 1: Raw Corn Cultivation

Natural corn crops are collected as the renewable organic raw base source, offering a fully sustainable supply chain footprint with low initial emissions.

Phase 2: Corn Starch Extraction

Corn elements undergo structural processing to isolate pure starch carbohydrates, removing non-essential plant fibers and proteins safely.

Phase 3: Polymer Synthesis (PLA & PBAT Granules)

Starch inputs are processed and synthesized into compostable raw material granules using organic PLA (Polylactic Acid) and PBAT structural polymers.

Phase 4: Advanced Bio Polymer Manufacturing

Granules are processed through our automated plant lines—blown, cut, and structurally sealed into highly resilient, functional eco-friendly carrier units.

Phase 5: 180-Day Pure Biodegradation

Post-disposal, under standard composting parameters, the material decomposes entirely back into natural nutrient-rich organic biomass, water, and CO2 within 180 days with zero toxic residue or microplastics.
